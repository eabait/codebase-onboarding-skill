Analyst Observations

Overall ranking: Claude baseline (96.8) > Codex CLI / gpt-5.2 (92.2) ≈ Gemini CLI (91.7). Both open harnesses land within ~5 points of the Claude baseline — a strong result for fully automated, non-Claude generation.

Citation density: Codex produces dramatically more inline source citations (~39.6 per page) than Gemini (~6.3 per page). This is architectural: Codex is agentic and reads files one at a time, citing as it goes. Gemini receives a pre-built prompt and cites more selectively. For engineering teams who rely on wiki citations to navigate code, Codex output is significantly more traceable.

Transparency markers: Flask and express runs score full marks (10/10) on transparency for both harnesses. Axios is the outlier — neither Codex nor Gemini added [NEEDS INVESTIGATION] markers for axios, suggesting both models are overconfident on that codebase. Claude scores 10/10 across all three repos.

Structural compliance: Both harnesses consistently hit full marks on Mermaid diagrams (15/15) and tables (10/10). The skill's explicit structural requirements propagate reliably across all three models — a direct validation of prompt design.

Depth vs breadth: Gemini produces shorter, cleaner pages (~5,400 words total per repo); Codex produces denser content (~8,900 words); Claude writes the deepest wikis (~16,200 words). Gemini is faster to read; Codex and Claude are better for deep architectural reference.

Recommendation: Codex CLI with gpt-5.2 is the strongest non-Claude alternative — within 4.6 points of baseline and superior on citation depth. Gemini is competitive and faster to run. For production wikis, Claude remains the highest-quality generator. For CI pipelines or cost-sensitive workflows, Codex gpt-5.2 is the recommended fallback.
